## 🔎 Case Study Analysis

### 🔴 Description of the Crime
The **"Request Money"** or **"Reverse QR Code"** scam is a deceptive financial fraud prevalent in India. It exploits the "Collect Request" feature on UPI platforms like PhonePe, GPay, and Paytm to trick users into unknowingly authorizing a withdrawal from their own bank accounts.

### ⚙️ What Typically Happens
1. **The Setup:** A scammer contacts the victim (often through OLX or Facebook Marketplace) pretending to be a buyer interested in an item.
2. **The Hook:** The scammer claims they want to pay an "advance" and sends a QR code via WhatsApp.
3. **The Trick:** They instruct the victim to **"Scan this code to receive the payment."**
4. **The Theft:** When the victim scans the code, the UPI app prompts for a **PIN**. Believing this is necessary to "verify" the receipt of funds, the victim enters their PIN—instantly transferring money *out* of their account to the scammer.

### 🎯 Who is Usually Targeted
* **Online Sellers:** Individuals selling used goods on secondary marketplaces.
* **Small Vendors:** Business owners who have recently transitioned to digital payments.
* **Students & Seniors:** Demographic groups who may be less familiar with the specific "Send vs. Receive" protocols of UPI.

### 📊 Impact and Analysis
The impact goes beyond the immediate financial loss; it creates a deep-seated fear of digital banking. This is a classic example of **Social Engineering**. Technically, the UPI apps are secure, but scammers bypass security by manipulating human trust. As an ambassador, the core message is simple: **A PIN is a "key" to your vault—you never need a key to let someone put money IN.**
